Salt Typhoon: The Hack That Won't Die
The Chinese hacker collective dubbed "Salt Typhoon" continues to haunt major US telecom providers including AT&T, Verizon, and T-Mobile. Intelligence officials confirm the group has maintained persistent access to some networks for over 18 months, intercepting communications of government officials and activists. The scope? Officially described as "the most significant telecom breach in US history."
THREAT LEVEL: CRITICAL

PowerSchool: 50M+ Students & Teachers Exposed
Education giant PowerSchool suffered a catastrophic breach in early 2026. Attackers exfiltrated personal data of over 50 million students and 6.5 million teachers — including SSNs, medical records, and grades. The attacker reportedly used a single compromised credential. Classic case of "no MFA = no mercy."
THREAT LEVEL: HIGH
SOURCE: POWERSCHOOL ADVISORY — FEB 2026
AI THREAT
AI-Powered Phishing Up 340% in 2026
Security researchers at CrowdStrike report a 340% spike in AI-generated phishing emails since last year. LLMs are now being used to craft hyper-personalized spear-phishing messages that bypass traditional filters. Even seasoned IT pros have been fooled.
THREAT LEVEL: HIGH
SOURCE: CROWDSTRIKE Q1 2026 REPORT
PRIVACY
DeepSeek AI Exposed User Data & Prompts
Chinese AI startup DeepSeek was found storing user conversations in an unprotected database accessible from the internet. Researchers discovered chat logs, API keys, and backend system data — a textbook example of insecure default configurations. Regulators in Italy and Ireland opened probes immediately.

GHOST PROTOCOL — TECH DEEP DIVE 06 / 07
THE RISE OF AI-POWERED CYBERATTACKS:
WHY YOUR ANTIVIRUS IS CRYING ITSELF TO SLEEP
ANALYSIS BY GHOST | CYPHER CLUB RESEARCH | MARCH 2026

We are living in the golden age of cyber threats — and not in a good way. Artificial intelligence has officially become the Swiss Army knife of the criminal underground, democratizing hacking to the point where your average script kiddie now has access to tools that would have made APT groups jealous five years ago.

"The sophistication gap between nation-state actors and cybercriminals is closing at an alarming rate. What took a state-sponsored team six months in 2020 can now be accomplished by a $50/month AI subscription in 2026."
— CrowdStrike Global Threat Report, Q1 2026

AI-Powered Phishing: Generative AI tools have made phishing campaigns terrifyingly effective. Attackers now use LLMs to scrape LinkedIn, company websites, and social media to craft hyper-personalized emails that reference real projects, real colleagues, and even mimic writing styles. The result? Click rates on malicious links have reportedly tripled since 2024.

Automated Vulnerability Discovery: Tools like AI and underground derivatives of major LLMs are being used to automatically scan codebases, identify zero-days, and generate working exploits — all with minimal human input. The cycle from "discovered vulnerability" to "weaponized exploit" has shrunk from weeks to hours.

Deepfake Social Engineering: The PowerSchool attackers reportedly used a deepfake voice call to impersonate an IT vendor's account manager. Voice cloning, real-time video deepfakes, and AI-generated "trusted faces" are becoming standard tools in the social engineer's arsenal. We're entering an era where "I heard your voice on the call" is no longer enough verification.
